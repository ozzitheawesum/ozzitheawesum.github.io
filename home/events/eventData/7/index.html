NUST Youth Parliament

PKR 500

Description keyboard_arrow_down

Youth parliaments provide a space for the politically conscious to express their ideas and propose solutions for real world problems. Such simulations prepare our young for the responsibilities and burdens of public office that will eventually fall on their shoulders. Keeping in view NUST Olympiad’s commitment to creating a more politically, socially aware society. We’re introducing the NUST Youth Parliament; a forum for debate, diplomacy and tolerance.

Rules keyboard_arrow_down

1. Language The official and working language of the conference will be English. 2. Representation Each member state will be represented by one delegate in each committee and shall have one vote on each committee. The Secretariat will provide a list of member states (political parties in case of PCC) for each committee to the Chair which may be disclosed on request. 3. Official Announcements The Secretary General, or any assigned member of the Secretariat, may make either written or oral statements to a committee at any point in time. 4. Authorities of the Chair 4.1. The Chair will declare the opening and closing of each committee session. 4.2. The Chair will have complete authority over the proceedings at committee sessions. 4.3. The Chair will: 4.3.1. direct debate and discussions; 4.3.2. accord the right to speak; 4.3.3. put questions and announce decisions; 4.3.4. rule on points of order; and 4.3.5. ensure and enforce the adherence to rules of procedure. 4.4. The Chair has the right to temporarily transfer his or her powers to another member of committee staff. 4.5. The Chair may advise or guide the delegates on course of debate. 4.6. Chairs will be subject to these rules at all times and will be answerable to the Secretariat. 6. Appeals Decisions of the Chair, except for the matters for which NUST Youth Parliament prohibits appeal, may be appealed by a delegate. 5.1. Procedure: 5.1.1. The Chair may speak in defense of the decision. 5.1.2. The appeal will then be put to a vote. 5.1.3. Decision of the Chair will stand unless overruled by a two-thirds majority. 5.2. Discretion: 5.2.1. The Chair has ultimate discretion on any decision, whether it is appealed successfully or not. 6. Quorum 6.1. Quorum is the number of delegates required to be present in order for the Chair to declare a session open and allow the proceedings to begin. 6.2. Quorum will be achieved when at least one-third of the committee members are present. 7. Code of Conduct 7.1. Delegates will show courtesy and respect to other delegates, to the members of Secretariat and to the organizing committee of NUST Youth Parliament. 7.2. Delegates will be required to adhere to the disciplinary and security policies of NUST Youth Parliament. 7.3. The Chair has the right to immediately call to order any delegate who fails to comply with this rule, and will take appropriate actions. 9. Use of Electronic Devices NUST Youth Parliament allows the delegates to use laptops, cell phones, electronic notebooks and tablets strictly for research purposes during a committee session. The Chair, however, reserves the right to disallow the use of electronic devices or to make modifications to this rule. 10. Amendments to Rules of Procedure The Chairs have the right to modify the rules of procedure, except for the rules for which NUST Youth Parliament prohibits modifications. The Secretary-General has the ultimate discretion over the modifications proposed by Chairs.
