Snooker (Boys)

PKR 500

Description keyboard_arrow_down

Are you good with angles? Is 8 your lucky number? Find out on this table of green! Test your patience with acute shots, precision with the white ball, and revise your laws of momentum because Olympiad’s got all the tables set for you.

Rules keyboard_arrow_down

  • Game of snooker is played between two players. The objective of the game is to win more number of frames than the opponent and to win a frame, the player should score more points. The player who successfully wins more number of frames, wins. Games usually played are best of 11, 15 or 17 frames. Potting:
  • To score a point, the player must hit the white ball known as cue ball with a wooden stick known as cue, to make that ball go into any of the pockets on the table. This is termed as potting the ball. Points of the balls:
  • Type Of The Ball Point(s)
  • Red ball (7) 1
  • Yellow Ball (1) 2
  • Green Ball (1) 3
  • Brown Ball (1) 4
  • Blue Ball (1) 5
  • Pink Ball (1) 6
  • Black Ball (1) 7
  • Rules for potting:
  • The player must pot the red ball first into any of the pockets, and then he is given a free choice to pot any of the other colored ball and gets the relevant points if he succeeds in doing so. If he fails, the turn is given to the other player and the process repeats.
  • Once all the red balls have been potted, the player must aim the colored balls in ascending order.
  • Fouls:
  • The player must not touch the ball with any other thing except the cue.
  • The player must not pot the cue ball accidently or otherwise.
  • The player must not touch an incorrect colored ball out of turn.
  • The player must not hit the ball off the table.
  • The player must not touch the cue ball twice in one stroke.
  • The player must not move a touching ball.
  • The player must not make a shot without hitting the required the ball.
  • The player must not make a new shot until all the balls settle completely.
  • The player must not play a shot with both feet off the ground.
  • The cue ball must not leave the bed of the table without hitting the ‘on’ ball.
  • The above mentioned fouls will result in awarding of 4 points and the next shot. If a foul involves blue, pink or black balls, 5,6 and 7 points are awarded respectively.
  • Terms to be Remembered:
  • 1. Cannon:
  • A cannon Is when the player hits the cue ball to contact more than one balls. This is done strategically to make the position of the cue ball favorable for the next shot.
  • 2. Touching ball:
  • If as a result of a shot, the cue ball settles in such a way that it is in contact with another ball, the referee will call touching ball. The must play the shot without moving this ball otherwise it would be a foul.
  • 3. Snookered:
  • The player may strategically hit the cue ball so as to make it difficult for the opponent to hit the next required ball. When a ball is blocking a shot to the next required ball, it is difficult to hit that ball. This is termed as being snookered.
  • 4. Foul and a miss:
  • If a players fails to hit a required ball, the opponent will get 4 points or more and a choice to play the next shot where the ball lies or have the balls reset to the previous positions and make the offending player try that shot again.
  • 5. Conceding:
  • If a player thinks that there are not realistic chances for him to win or realizes that there are not enough points on the table, the player can concede defeat and a new frame will start.
  • 6. Free ball:
  • If a red ball is snookered as a result of a foul and the cue ball can’t hit it, then the affected player is given a choice to nominate any other colored ball and hit it as if it was a red ball and get one point.
  • The nominated ball is then repositioned to its previous location.
  • 7. Maximum Break (147):
  • The maximum score in one turn is 147 points. This is done by potting a red ball, then potting a black ball, repeating this process 15 times and then proceeding to pot the remaining colored balls in order without committing a foul. If a player manages to do it, it is termed as maximum break or a 147.
